Sparks

Sparks represent data packages that come into the CPU (input sparks) and out from the CPU (output sparks). There are several types of sparks (for both input and output sparks). Some are common, some are rare:

A good spark (common). Scanners and towers should let this spark through undamaged.

A corrupted spark (common). You scanners should mark corrupted sparks, so that towers can kill them.

A virus (rare). Virus is a dangerous corrupted spark that can disable your towers.

A firewall spark (rare). This special type of corrupted spark protects other sparks from being shot by towers.

Scanners will mark sparks that do not meet their programmed conditions. Additionally towers can have upgrades that add effects on the sparks. Such an effect can only be applied to a spark that a tower shoots at (so, unmarked sparks will never get an effect).

A marked good spark. Towers will shoot at it. This is not good.

A marked corrupted spark. Towers will shoot at it. This is good.

A bomb good spark. Some tower effect can turn the spark shot by the tower to a bomb that damages other marked sparks when it dies. Turning a good spark to a bomb is not good.

A bomb corrupted spark. Some tower effect can turn the spark shot by the tower to a bomb that damages other marked sparks when it dies. Turning a corrupted spark to a bomb is good.

A slowed good spark. Some tower effect can slow the spark shot by the tower. Slowing a good spark is not good.

A slowed corrupted spark. Some tower effect can slow the spark shot by the tower. Slowing a corrupted spark is good.
